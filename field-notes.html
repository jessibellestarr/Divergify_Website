Field Notes

This is where we publish the thinking. Not motivational posters. Actual observations about cognition, friction, and behavior. Short, clean, useful.

1. Procrastination is often threat detection

When a task feels unsafe, vague, or socially risky, your nervous system treats it like danger. Avoidance is not a character flaw. It is a protective response. The fix is usually to reduce ambiguity and shrink the first step until it feels safe to start.

2. Willpower is a terrible primary strategy

If your plan depends on constant self control, it will fail on the first high stress day. Structure is what survives reality. Environment design beats self blame every time.

3. Calm UI is not softness

Calm surfaces reduce cognitive load. Lower interference means more bandwidth for decision making. That is not “gentle.” That is tactical.
